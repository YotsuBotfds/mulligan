Boiling Water

Boiling is the most reliable water purification method available in survival situations. Rolling boil kills virtually all pathogens including bacteria, viruses, and parasites.

Boiling Procedure

  • Bring water to a vigorous rolling boil (not just hot water)
  • Maintain rolling boil for minimum 1 minute at sea level
  • At elevations above 6,500 feet, boil for 3 minutes due to lower water temperature
  • Allow water to cool before drinking
  • Store in clean, sealed containers

Effectiveness

Boiling eliminates: bacteria (E. coli, typhoid, cholera), viruses (hepatitis A, rotavirus), protozoa (Giardia, Cryptosporidium), and helminths. It does NOT remove chemical contaminants, heavy metals, or sediment.

Fuel Efficiency

To conserve fuel: use minimal water amount needed, cover pot to retain heat, use dead wood efficiently, pre-filter water to remove sediment before boiling, collect morning dew for drinking (already pure from condensation).

Filtration Methods

Filtration removes sediment, pathogens, and improves water clarity. Multiple filtration stages work together for best results.

Bio-Sand Filter Construction

A bio-sand filter is a multi-layer filtration system that removes 99% of pathogens without chemicals:

Layer-by-Layer Construction

  1. Fine Sand (5-10cm): Primary filtration layer. Use sand with grain size 0.5-1mm. Creates biofilm layer after 1-2 weeks that removes pathogens through biological action and mechanical filtration.
  2. Coarse Sand (5-10cm): Supports fine sand, allows water flow. Prevent mixing of layers by carefully adding.
  3. Gravel (5cm): Supports sand layers, prevents fine sand from escaping to output chamber.
  4. Support Layer: Plastic mesh or fabric above gravel to prevent disturbance during water addition.

Sand Filter Specifications

Sand should be uniform grain size. Test by filling bucket with sand, adding water, letting settle overnight. Good sand will show clear layers. Poor sand with too much silt will cloud water.

Cloth Filtration

Simple method for sediment removal: Layer clean cloth (cotton or linen) in funnel, pour water through slowly. Removes large particles but NOT pathogens. Must be combined with boiling or chemical treatment.

Charcoal Filtration

Activated charcoal removes chemical contaminants, improves taste and smell. Make by burning wood in low-oxygen fire, crushing, then rinsing in water. Layer 5-10cm charcoal between sand layers. Replace every 2 weeks as charcoal becomes saturated.

Ceramic Filter Elements

If available, ceramic filter tubes with 0.2 micron pores remove 99.99% of bacteria and protozoa. Flow rate approximately 1 liter per hour. Clean by back-flushing with clean water regularly. Do not let dry out between uses as it damages ceramic structure.

Chemical Treatment

Chemical methods rapidly disinfect water without heat requirement. Effective against bacteria and viruses, variable against protozoa.

Chlorine Dioxide

Most effective chemical treatment available. Tablets typically dissolve in water creating dioxide gas that kills pathogens within 30 minutes at typical doses. 2-3 tablets per liter depending on water clarity. Follow package instructions carefully. Does NOT work well in pH above 8.5.

Iodine Treatment

Two-tablet solution: each tablet contains 8mg iodine. For clear water, use 2 tablets per liter, wait 30 minutes. For cloudy water, use 4 tablets per liter, wait 4 hours. NOT safe for pregnant women, iodine-allergic individuals, or extended use beyond 3-6 months. Can cause thyroid issues with long-term use.

Potassium Permanganate

Removes iron, manganese, and improves taste. NOT a standalone disinfectant. Dose approximately 1mg per liter (water turns light pink). Wait 5 minutes then filter. Follow with iodine or boiling for complete disinfection.

Mixing Chemical Methods

Can combine iodine and permanganate. Add permanganate first, wait 5 minutes, then iodine. Do not exceed iodine dosing recommendations when combining methods.

WARNING: Do not exceed recommended iodine doses over extended periods. High iodine accumulation causes thyroid dysfunction. Pregnant women should not use iodine treatment. Alternatives (boiling or chlorine dioxide) recommended for vulnerable populations.

Solar Disinfection (SODIS)

Uses UV radiation to destroy pathogens without chemicals or fuel. Simple but requires time and sunlight availability.

SODIS Method

  1. Fill clear plastic bottle (2-3 liters) with water
  2. Place in direct sunlight
  3. Leave exposed for minimum 6 hours (full day recommended)
  4. Water is safe to drink after UV exposure

Effectiveness

UV radiation damages DNA of bacteria, viruses, and most protozoa. Extremely effective against bacteria and viruses. Less reliable against Cryptosporidium oocysts which have thicker protective shells. Combined solar + boiling gives maximum confidence.

Enhanced Solar Method

Accelerate disinfection by adding reflective materials. Line box with aluminum foil, angle bottles toward sun. Can achieve full disinfection in 2-3 hours with enhanced reflectivity.

Limitations

Requires clear plastic (not opaque containers), consistent sunlight, stable outdoor location, minimum 4-6 hour exposure time. Does not remove sediment or chemical contaminants.

Rainwater Collection

Rainwater is naturally relatively pure, requiring minimal treatment compared to surface water sources.

Collection System Basics

  • First rainfall washes atmospheric dust and roof debris - discard initial 10-20 gallons if possible
  • Use clean collection surface (clean tarp, roof gutters)
  • Direct into clean, sealed containers
  • Screen large debris with cloth or mesh
  • Still requires boiling or chemical treatment for safety

Catchment Area Calculation

One inch of rainfall on 1,000 square feet of catchment surface = approximately 600 gallons of water. Typical roof catchment yields 500-600 gallons per inch of rainfall depending on roof material and angle.

Container Selection

Use food-grade containers only. Do not use containers previously holding chemicals, pesticides, or non-food items. Large containers (55-gallon drums) preferred for storage. Opaque containers prevent algae growth.

Pre-Filtration Before Storage

Pour collected rainwater through cloth screen to remove leaves and debris. Simple gravity filtration through sand dramatically improves quality before final disinfection treatment.

Storage Duration

Properly stored rainwater remains safe for months in cool, dark conditions. Cover containers tightly to prevent mosquito breeding and dust contamination. Change stored water every 6 months if possible.

Well Construction Basics

Deep groundwater sources provide better quality than surface water, but require proper construction to prevent contamination.

Well Site Selection

  • Locate at least 50 feet downslope from latrine, compost, or manure piles
  • At least 100 feet from septic systems
  • In areas where groundwater naturally flows through
  • Avoid low-lying areas where surface water collects
  • Test for adequate year-round water availability

Simple Dug Well Construction

  1. Excavation: Dig hole 4-6 feet deep in areas with high water table. Deeper wells provide better quality. Use digging tools or bucket-lifting if no machinery available.
  2. Lining: Line well with stones or PVC pipe to prevent cave-in and contamination. Stones should be large, firmly placed.
  3. Screening: Install cloth or screen at bottom to filter sediment before water enters collection chamber.
  4. Seal Top: Cap well with fitted lid. Leave only small opening for bucket access. Prevents leaf fall and animal contamination.
  5. Surround Protection: Create raised berm of compacted soil around well opening. Prevents runoff water from draining into well.

Well Water Treatment

Even deep wells require treatment. Groundwater may contain iron, sulfur compounds, or pathogens. Minimum treatment: sand filtration + boiling or chemical disinfection. Test well water annually if possible.

Maintenance

Clean well monthly by removing sediment that accumulates at bottom. Scrub interior surfaces annually. If water becomes cloudy, stop use and allow settlement for 2-3 days before resuming. If water develops foul smell, well is contaminated - relocate well or implement heavy treatment.

TIP: Traditional communities used slow-sand wells (wide, shallow) that naturally filter water through sand layers. Highly effective when properly maintained.

Common Mistakes

  • Incomplete Boiling: Water at 80°C looks like boiling but may not kill all pathogens. Maintain rolling boil, not just hot water.
  • Skipping Pre-Filtration: Cloudiness reduces chemical treatment effectiveness. Always pre-filter debris before chemical or iodine treatment.
  • Over-Relying on Single Method: Combine methods for confidence. Boiling + filtration + storage provides margin of safety.
  • Insufficient Iodine Wait Time: Cold water requires full 30+ minute wait time. Don't drink immediately after adding tablets.
  • Contaminating Clean Water: After purification, use separate clean container for storage. Don't dip dirty cup into purified water.
  • Forgetting Sediment Removal: Sand in water blocks UV from SODIS and reduces chemical effectiveness. Filter before final treatment.
  • Long-Term Iodine Use: Extended iodine treatment (months) causes thyroid problems. Rotate methods or switch to boiling.
