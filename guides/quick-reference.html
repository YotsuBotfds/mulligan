CPR Quick Reference

Adult

30
Compressions
2
Breaths
Depth: 2 inches
Rate: 100-120/min
Place heel of hand on center of chest

Child

Same 30:2 ratio
Depth: 1.5 inches

Infant

2 fingers, 1.5 inches deep
Same 30:2 ratio
Call emergency services immediately

Choking Response

Conscious Adult

5
Back Blows
5
Abdominal Thrusts
Repeat until object dislodges or person collapses

Abdominal Thrust (Heimlich)

Stand behind victim
Place fist above navel, below ribcage
Grasp fist with other hand
Quick upward thrust

Unconscious Victim

Begin CPR
Check mouth before each rescue breath
Remove object if visible

Infant (Under 1 Year)

5 back blows (face-down)
5 chest thrusts (2 fingers)
Check mouth, repeat

Severe Bleeding Control

Step 1: Direct Pressure

Apply pressure with clean cloth
Hold for 10-15 minutes
DO NOT remove cloth, just add more on top

Step 2: Elevation

Raise wounded area above heart

Step 3: Tourniquet (Last Resort)

Use ONLY if bleeding won't stop
Place 2 inches above wound
Tighten until bleeding stops
WRITE TIME on tourniquet marker
NEVER remove tourniquet
Get emergency medical help NOW

Pressure Points

Inner arm (brachial artery)
Inner groin (femoral artery)
Neck side (carotid artery)

ORS (Oral Rehydration)

Life-Saving Formula

1 Liter Clean Water
6 Teaspoons Sugar
½ Teaspoon Salt

Instructions

Mix ingredients thoroughly
Sip slowly and continuously
Small, frequent amounts work best

Use For

Severe diarrhea
Vomiting
Dehydration
Heat exhaustion

Critical Note

CAN SAVE LIVES in severe dehydration
Start rehydration immediately when symptoms appear
Continue for 24-48 hours minimum

Fire Starting Checklist

Layer 1: Tinder

Dry, fingernail-sized pieces
Dry leaves, bark, paper
Must be very fine and dry

Layer 2: Kindling

Pencil-thick sticks
Arrange in teepee shape
Leave center open for tinder

Layer 3: Fuel

Wrist-thick logs
Add after kindling catches

Critical Setup

Shield from wind
Ground underneath must be clear
Water source nearby

Ignition

Light tinder center
Blow gently at base to spread flame
Add kindling as tinder burns

Shelter Priorities

Priority Order

1. Get OFF ground
2. Block WIND
3. INSULATE from cold

Key Principle

SMALLER space = WARMER shelter
Don't make it too large
Just big enough to lie down

Debris Hut

Lean sticks against ridgepole
Pile leaves 3 feet thick
Cover floor with insulation
Create small entrance only

Emergency Materials

Leaves, pine needles, bark
Moss, grass, clothing
Anything between you and ground

Rule of 3s

Survival priority sequence:

3 Minutes without air/oxygen
3 Hours without shelter
(in extreme weather)
3 Days without water
3 Weeks without food

What This Means

AIR is the absolute first priority
SHELTER before water in extreme conditions
WATER before food (longer survival window)
FOOD has the longest window
Manage priorities in order of urgency

Universal Edibility Test

For unknown plants only. High failure risk.

1. Separate plant parts (roots, stems, leaves)
2. Smell — reject if foul
3. Skin contact test 15 min
4. Lip contact test 15 min
5. Tongue contact test 15 min
6. Chew, spit, wait 15 min
7. Swallow small amount, wait 8 hours
8. Eat larger amount, wait 24 hours
If ANY symptom appears, induce vomiting and seek help

Danger Signs

Nausea, cramping, diarrhea
Itching lips or mouth
Burning sensation
Milky sap or umbrella-shaped flowers (avoid)

Essential Knots

1. Bowline
Creates fixed loop; won't slip. Perfect for rescue.
2. Clove Hitch
Quick tie to post or tree; easy to untie.
3. Taut-Line Hitch
Adjustable loop that holds under tension.
4. Square Knot
Join two ropes of equal diameter. Right over left.
5. Figure-8 Knot
Stopper knot at end of rope. Won't slip through.
6. Trucker's Hitch
Mechanical advantage knot for heavy loads.

Burns Treatment

1st Degree (Red, No Blistering)

Cool with water 10-20 min
Apply aloe vera gel
Pain relief as needed

2nd Degree (Blistered, Red)

Cool with water immediately
Do NOT pop blisters
Cover loosely with clean cloth
Antibiotic ointment optional

3rd Degree (Charred, White, Deep)

DO NOT apply water to large areas
Cover with CLEAN cloth only
Treat for shock (lay flat, elevate legs)
Emergency medical help URGENT

All Burns

Remove tight jewelry and clothing
Monitor for airway obstruction
Watch for signs of shock
Seek medical help for burns larger than 3 inches
