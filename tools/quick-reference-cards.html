Water Purification Quick Ref
BOILING TIMES (altitude)
  • <6,500 ft: 1 minute
  • 6,500-8,500 ft: 3 minutes
  • >8,500 ft: 5+ minutes
BLEACH TREATMENT
  • Clear water: 2 drops/qt
  • Cloudy water: 4 drops/qt

FILTER LAYERS (top→bottom)

  • 4. Gravel

SODIS (Solar)
  • Clear water in clear bottle
  • Full sun: 6+ hours
  • Cloudy: 2+ days

Edible Plant ID
UNIVERSAL SAFE PLANTS
  • Dandelion: Jagged leaves, yellow flower, milky stem
  • Cattail: Brown top, long blade leaves, aquatic
  • Clover: Round leaflet clusters, 3-lobed
  • Chickweed: Small opposite leaves, weak stem
  • Plantain: Oval leaves in rosette, parallel veins
  • Purslane: Fleshy red stems, thick leaves
  • Watercress: Small round leaves, peppery taste
  • Acorns: Oak cap, boil 5+ times to remove tannins
  • Pine nuts: Soft nuts inside cone
  • Birch sap: Spring, mild sweet taste
UNIV. EDIBILITY TEST
  • 1. Break skin, smell
  • 2. Touch to wrist (reaction?)
  • 3. Touch to lip (15 min)
  • 4. Chew, spit out (5 min)
  • 5. Swallow 1/4 tsp (5 hr wait)
  • 6. Eat 1 oz (24 hr wait)
Dangerous Plants (Avoid!)
LOOKALIKES TO AVOID
  • Water Hemlock: vs. Wild carrot - hollow stems, purple blotches, musty smell TOXIC
  • Poison Hemlock: vs. Wild parsnip - 6+ ft tall, hairless, red blotches TOXIC
  • Deadly Nightshade: Bell flowers, black berries, no smell TOXIC
  • Foxglove: Tall spire, fuzzy leaves, pink/purple flowers CARDIAC
  • Larkspur: vs. Delphinium - purple/blue, spur on back TOXIC
  • Poison Oak: 3 leaflets, glossy, no thorns DERMATITIS
  • Poison Ivy: 3 leaflets, glossy, center stalk longer DERMATITIS
  • Stinging Nettle: Fuzzy, stinging hairs, square stem CONTACT
  • Castor Bean: Large leaves, spiky pods, no taste RICIN POISON
  • Ergot (grain fungus): Black spur on cereal crops GANGRENOUS

First Aid Priorities (MARCH)
MARCH PROTOCOL
  • M - Massive hemorrhage: Apply tourniquets above wound
  • A - Airway: Clear mouth, reposition head
  • R - Respirations: Check breathing, start rescue breaths
  • C - Circulation: Check pulse, CPR if needed
  • H - Hypothermia: Handle gently, rewarm slowly
BLEEDING CONTROL
  • 1. Direct pressure (clean cloth)
  • 2. Elevate limb above heart
  • 3. Pressure points (artery)
  • 4. Tourniquet (2" above wound)
  • 5. Write time on tourniquet
SHOCK TREATMENT
  • Lay flat, elevate legs 12"
  • Keep warm (blanket)
  • Monitor breathing & pulse
  • Don't give food/water
SPLINTING BASICS
  • Immobilize joint above/below
  • Pad splint with cloth
  • Check circulation after
  • Keep elevated, cold compress
Trapping Quick Ref
SNARE TYPES & DIMS
  • Figure-4 deadfall: Bait trigger, heavy log, rabbit-sized
  • Paiute deadfall: No bait, self-setting, larger game
  • Spring snare: Sapling bent, noose at trail height
  • Loop snare: Simple brass wire, neck-height, 4" diameter
BAIT BY ANIMAL
  • Rabbit: clover, bark, twigs
  • Squirrel: nuts, seeds, berries
  • Deer: salt, acorns, browse
  • Fish: insects, worms, smaller fish
PLACEMENT TIPS
  • Set on trails, water crossings
  • Use natural funneling
  • Disguise human scent
  • Check traps daily
  • Set multiple traps
Navigation
FINDING NORTH
  • Stick shadow: N-S line, shorter shadow = south
  • North Star: Find Big Dipper, follow pointer stars
  • Watch method: Point hour hand at sun, bisect to 12 = S
  • Moon: Terminator line points north
PACE COUNTING
  • Count every other step
  • Measure your pace (flat, level)
  • Typical: 30-40 paces = 100 ft
  • Adjust for terrain, load
BASIC TRIANGULATION
  • 1. Sight known landmark (bearing)
  • 2. Sight second landmark
  • 3. Intersection = position
  • Use compass or sun angles
Food Preservation Times/Temps
SMOKING
  • Cold: 70-90°F, 12-24 hrs
  • Hot: 160-180°F, 4-8 hrs
  • Smoke woods: oak, hickory, apple
DRYING
  • Sun: 60-80°F, 24-72 hrs
  • Shade: lower heat, slower
  • Brittle to touch = done
  • Store in dry, cool place
SALT CURING
  • 1:3 meat-to-salt ratio
  • Rub meat thoroughly
  • Store in cool, dry
  • Lasts months to years
FERMENTATION
  • Salt: 2-5% by weight
  • Keep submerged under brine
  • Room temp 60-70°F
  • 3-6 weeks to ferment
CANNING PRESSURE
  • Low altitude: 10-15 psi
  • 3000+ ft: 20+ psi
  • Process time: 30-90 min
Forging Temperature Colors
COLOR-TO-TEMP CHART
  • Black heat: 400-700°F, unworkable
  • Dark red: 700-800°F, hard to see
  • Bright cherry red: 800-900°F, slow working
  • Cherry red: 900-1000°F, good starting
  • Bright cherry: 1000-1100°F, optimal work
  • Orange: 1100-1200°F, final work
  • Yellow-orange: 1200-1300°F, near finish
  • Yellow: 1300-1400°F, very hot
  • Light yellow: 1400-1500°F, sparking
  • White heat: 1500°F+, oxidation risk
QUENCH MEDIA
  • Water: fast, brittle risk
  • Oil: slower, safer
  • Brine: balanced
TEMPER COLORS
  • Pale yellow: 300°F, very hard
  • Straw: 350°F, good edge
  • Brown: 400°F, balanced
  • Light blue: 600°F, flexible
Wood Species Guide
15 COMMON TREES
  • Oak: Very hard, slow burn, excellent fuel, rot resistant
  • Maple: Hard, dense, good coals, less rot resistant
  • Ash: Hard, straight grain, splits easy, good burning
  • Hickory: Very hard, dense, excellent coals, great tool wood
  • Birch: Medium hard, white inner bark, splits poorly
  • Pine: Soft, resinous, sparks, fast fire, good bow wood
  • Cedar: Soft, aromatic, rot resistant, good shingles
  • Spruce: Soft, light, resonant, good bows, weak fire
  • Elm: Hard, bends well, splits poorly, waterproof
  • Walnut: Hard, dark, durable, excellent turning
  • Poplar: Very soft, light, poor fire, excellent spindle
  • Willow: Soft, flexible, splits easy, friction fire bow
  • Basswood: Soft, light, straight grain, excellent spindle
  • Locust: Very hard, dense, excellent durability
  • Apple: Hard, dense, smoking wood, strong structure
Cordage Quick Ref
REVERSE WRAP STEPS
  • 1. Strip bark from inner fiber
  • 2. Soak 24+ hrs to soften
  • 3. Beat to separate fibers
  • 4. Twist 2+ fibers together
  • 5. Ply cords into rope
FIBER SOURCES
  • Bark: willow, basswood, cedar
  • Leaves: yucca, iris, palm
  • Plant: nettle, milkweed, hemp
  • Inner bark: wet separates best
BREAKING STRENGTHS
  • Natural 1" rope: 3,000-5,000 lbs
  • Twisted bark: 500-1,500 lbs
  • Test your cordage!
SPLICE TECHNIQUE
  • Eye splice: loop end
  • Short splice: join two ropes
  • Whip end: prevent fraying
Animal Tracks
10 GAME ANIMALS
  • Deer: Heart shape, 2-3" wide, gentle toes, 24-48" stride
  • Elk: Larger than deer, 4" wide, 60"+ stride, rounded
  • Moose: Huge, 5-6" wide, splayed hooves, 80"+ stride
  • Bear: Hand-like (5 toes), 3-5" wide, deep claw marks
  • Raccoon: Hand-print, 2" wide, 5 toes visible, pigeon-toed
  • Squirrel: Tiny, 1/2" wide, 4 toes front/5 rear, hop pattern
  • Rabbit: 4-toed, hind foot longer, hopping pattern, 24-30" stride
  • Fox: Dog-like, 1.5" wide, straight line trail, claw marks
  • Coyote: Larger fox, 2" wide, very straight trail
  • Bobcat: 4-toed, 1.5" wide, retracted claws, rolling gait
Weather Prediction
CLOUD TYPES & MEANING
  • Cumulus: Puffy, flat base = fair weather
  • Cumulonimbus: Anvil top = severe storm coming
  • Stratus: Gray blanket = drizzle/overcast
  • Cirrus: Wispy, high = weather change in 24 hrs
  • Altocumulus: Rippled = afternoon thunderstorm
WIND SHIFTS & PRESSURE
  • Shift clockwise = improving weather
  • Shift counterclockwise = worsening
  • Increasing wind = low pressure → storms
  • Calm before storm = pressure drop
NATURAL SIGNS
  • Animals sheltering = weather change
  • Dew on grass = fair tomorrow
  • No dew = rain coming
  • Red sunrise = storm approach
  • Red sunset = fair tomorrow

Radio Frequencies
EMERGENCY FREQUENCIES
  • VHF 156.8 MHz: Maritime emergency (Channel 16)
  • VHF 121.5 MHz: Aircraft emergency
  • 2 meters: 146.52 MHz (simplex, local amateur)

FRS/GMRS CHANNELS
  • FRS: Ch 1-7 (short range, license-free)
  • GMRS: Ch 8-14 (longer range, license required)
  • Ch 1-8 available on both (FRS/GMRS)
  • Use 462-467 MHz range
SIGNAL PROTOCOLS
  • CQ: General call (seeking anyone)

  • RST: Readability, Signal, Tone
  • QSL: Acknowledge receipt
  • Phonetic alphabet for clarity

STROP TECHNIQUE
